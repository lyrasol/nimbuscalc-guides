Loan & Mortgage Guide: Understand Your Payments

Taking out a loan is a major financial decision. This guide explains how loan payments are calculated, what affects interest, and how to compare offers using a loan calculator.

How Monthly Payments Are Calculated

Most loans (mortgages, auto loans) use amortization: each payment covers interest for the period plus a portion of the principal. The formula is:

M = P × [ r(1+r)^n ] / [ (1+r)^n – 1 ]
Where M = monthly payment, P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, n = number of payments.

Our auto loan calculator and mortgage calculator do this math instantly, including taxes and fees.

Factors That Affect Your Loan

  • Interest rate: Higher rate = higher total cost. Fixed vs. variable rates matter.
  • Loan term: Longer terms mean lower monthly payments but more total interest.
  • Down payment: A larger down payment reduces principal and may lower the rate.
  • Credit score: Better scores often qualify for lower rates.

Example: Auto Loan

For a $25,000 car loan at 5% APR over 60 months, the monthly payment is about $472. Total interest paid over the term is around $3,300.
