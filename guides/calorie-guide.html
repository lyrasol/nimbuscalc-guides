Calorie & TDEE Guide: Find Your Perfect Energy Balance

Whether you want to lose weight, build muscle, or maintain, understanding your calorie needs is essential. This guide explains Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), and how to set calorie goals.

What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including all activities. It consists of:

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): calories burned at rest (breathing, circulation, etc.)
  • Physical activity: calories burned through movement and exercise
  • Thermic effect of food: calories used to digest and process food

How to Calculate Your TDEE

The most accurate method is using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which our free calorie calculator uses. Simply enter your age, gender, height, weight, and activity level, and it will give you your maintenance calories.

Example: A 30-year-old moderately active woman weighing 65 kg with height 165 cm has a TDEE of approximately 2,300 calories per day.

Setting Calorie Goals

  • To lose weight: Eat 300–500 calories below TDEE (safe rate of 0.5–1 kg per week).
  • To gain muscle: Eat 200–300 calories above TDEE with adequate protein.
  • To maintain: Eat at TDEE level.

Always aim for a balanced diet with enough protein, carbs, and healthy fats. You can use our macro calculator to get personalized recommendations, or explore protein calculator and water intake calculator for more specific goals.
